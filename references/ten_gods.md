# Ten Gods (十神)

Ten Gods describe what another stem (or hidden stem) *is* relative to the Day Master — always a two-step check:

1. **Relationship**: does the other stem generate the Day Master, get generated by it, control it, get controlled by it, or match it (五行生克)?
2. **Yin-Yang match**: is the other stem's Yin/Yang the *same* as the Day Master's, or *different*?

| Relationship | Same Yin-Yang | Different Yin-Yang |
|---|---|---|
| Controls Day Master (克我) | 七杀 (Seven Killings) | 正官 (Direct Officer) |
| Day Master controls (我克) | 偏财 (Indirect Wealth) | 正财 (Direct Wealth) |
| Generates Day Master (生我) | 偏印 (Indirect Resource) | 正印 (Direct Resource) |
| Day Master generates (我生) | 食神 (Eating God) | 伤官 (Hurting Officer) |
| Same as Day Master (同我) | 比肩 (Rob Wealth peer) | 劫财 (Rob Wealth) |

Grouped categories used in strength scoring:
- **帮身 (supports Day Master)**: 比肩, 劫财, 正印, 偏印
- **耗身 (drains Day Master)**: 正官, 七杀 (control — heaviest drain), 正财, 偏财 (Day Master exerts effort — medium-heavy drain), 食神, 伤官 (Day Master expresses/outputs — lighter, more "natural" drain)

Rough drain-intensity ordering (traditional consensus, not universal): **官杀 > 财 > 食伤** — being controlled costs the Day Master most, exerting control (acquiring wealth) costs somewhat less, and generating output (self-expression) costs least.

## Quick derivation example (Day Master = 庚, Yang Metal)

- 己 (Yin Earth) generates Metal, different Yin-Yang from 庚 → **正印**
- 丙 (Yang Fire) controls Metal, same Yin-Yang as 庚 → **七杀**
- 癸 (Yin Water), Metal generates Water, different Yin-Yang → **伤官**
- 乙 (Yin Wood), Metal controls Wood, different Yin-Yang → **正财**
- 甲 (Yang Wood), Metal controls Wood, same Yin-Yang → **偏财**
- 丁 (Yin Fire), controls Metal, different Yin-Yang → **正官**

To get the Ten God for *any* Day Master + any stem/hidden-stem, just run the two-step check above — don't try to memorize a full 10x10 table, the rule generalizes cleanly.
