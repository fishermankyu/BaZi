Main qi (本气) = the branch's dominant, always-present stem (largest share of days in that month).
Middle qi (中气) = present only in 寅申巳亥 (four long-life/transition branches).
Residual qi (余气) = the tail-end influence of the adjacent/prior element.

## Why the pattern looks the way it does

- **子卯酉** (and 午, mostly): only main qi (or main+one), because these are the "purest" months — no leftover transition needed. (午 uniquely carries a 己 middle qi.)
- **寅申巳亥** (四长生, season-starters): 3 qi each. Residual is **always 戊 (Yang Earth)** — Earth acts as a fixed buffer/mediator at the start of every new season, not tied to "what the previous season was."
- **辰戌丑未** (四库, season-closers): 3 qi each. Main is always the relevant Earth stem; middle+residual reflect the *tail of the season just ending* (e.g. 辰 closes spring → carries 乙 wood residual + 癸 water "vault" qi).

## Why 亥 has main qi 壬 (Yang) despite 亥 being a Yin branch

This is the twelve-stage life-cycle (十二长生) system layered on top — a *separate* system from the branch's own Yin-Yang label (see stems_branches.md). Yang and Yin stems of the same element travel the twelve-branch cycle in **opposite directions** (阳顺阴逆):

| Stem | 长生 (birth) | 帝旺 (peak) | 墓 (vault) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 甲 (Yang Wood) | 亥 | 卯 | 未 |
| 乙 (Yin Wood) | 午 | 寅 | 戌 |
| 丙/戊 (Yang Fire/Earth*) | 寅 | 午 | 戌 |
| 丁/己 (Yin Fire/Earth*) | 酉 | 巳 | 丑 |
| 庚 (Yang Metal) | 巳 | 酉 | 丑 |
| 辛 (Yin Metal) | 子 | 申 | 辰 |
| 壬 (Yang Water) | 申 | 子 | 辰 |
| 癸 (Yin Water) | 卯 | 亥 | 未 |

*戊/己 conventionally "borrow" 丙/丁's cycle (寄宫).

亥 sits at 壬's "临官" stage (just before its 帝旺 at 子) — a position of real, rising Yang-Water strength — which is why 亥's hidden main qi is 壬, not the branch's own nominal Yin label. This system explains the exceptions but is not needed to just look up the hidden-stems table above; treat it as background, not something to re-derive each time.
